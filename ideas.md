  **Design Movement**: **Modern Mumbai Pop Art**
  
  **Core Principles**:
  1. **Vibrant Chaos**: Embrace the organized chaos of Indian streets but with a clean, digital twist.
  2. **Bold Typography**: Use typography as a primary visual element, mimicking street signs and hand-painted truck art.
  3. **High Contrast**: Strong contrast between background and foreground to make content pop.
  4. **Playful Authenticity**: Use illustrations and graphics that feel hand-drawn or imperfect to reflect the "street" nature.

  **Color Philosophy**:
  - **Saffron Orange (#FF9933)**: Represents energy, spice, and the "Chai" element.
  - **Deep Teal (#006666)**: A cooling counterpoint to the spice, representing the "Thali" variety.
  - **Marigold Yellow (#FFCC00)**: For highlights and calls to action, evoking festivity.
  - **Off-White/Cream**: For backgrounds to soften the intensity and improve readability.
  - *Reasoning*: This palette captures the sensory explosion of Indian street food without being overwhelming on a screen.

  **Layout Paradigm**:
  - **Asymmetric Grid**: Break the standard grid with overlapping elements and off-center headings.
  - **Card-Based Staggering**: Menu items and sections appear in staggered cards rather than straight rows.
  - **Full-Width Visual Breaks**: Use large, bold typographic or illustrative sections to break up content.

  **Signature Elements**:
  - **Truck Art Motifs**: Floral patterns and decorative borders inspired by Indian truck art.
  - **Chai Glass Iconography**: Custom icons for menu categories (chai glass, samosa triangle, thali circle).
  - **Textured Backgrounds**: Subtle paper or wall textures to give a tactile feel.

  **Interaction Philosophy**:
  - **Hover Tilts**: Cards tilt slightly on hover, adding a playful, tactile dimension.
  - **Parallax Scrolling**: Background elements move at different speeds to create depth.
  - **Cursor Effects**: A custom cursor (perhaps a small spice sprinkle) or trail.

  **Animation**:
  - **Entrance**: Elements slide in from different directions (left, right, bottom) with a bouncy easing.
  - **Scroll**: Text reveals with a "paint-on" effect or character-by-character stagger.
  - **Micro-interactions**: Buttons pulse slightly; icons wiggle on hover.

  **Typography System**:
  - **Headings**: *Rozha One* (High contrast serif) or *Shrikhand* (Bold, retro Gujarati style) for a distinct Indian flavor.
  - **Body**: *Poppins* or *DM Sans* (Geometric sans-serif) for modern readability and clean lines.
  - **Accents**: *Caveat* (Handwriting style) for notes and "chef's specials".
</idea>

  - **Charcoal (#3D405B)**: For text, softer than black.
  - *Reasoning*: A palette that feels grounded, organic, and sophisticated, moving away from the stereotypical bright "curry house" colors.

  **Layout Paradigm**:
  - **Split Screen**: Vertical split screens for storytelling (image on one side, text on the other).
  - **Editorial Style**: Layouts that resemble a food magazine, with large images and elegant typography.
  - **Whitespace Dominance**: Generous margins and padding to create a sense of luxury and calm.

  **Signature Elements**:
  - **Mandala Outlines**: Very subtle, thin-line mandala patterns as background watermarks.
  - **Gold Foil Accents**: Use a gold gradient for borders or key headings to add a touch of premium feel.

  **Interaction Philosophy**:
  - **Smooth Fade-ins**: Content fades in gently as the user scrolls.
  - **Image Zoom**: Subtle zoom on images when hovered.
  - **Sticky Navigation**: A clean, minimal navigation bar that stays with the user.

  - **Slow & Graceful**: Transitions are slow and smooth (ease-in-out).
